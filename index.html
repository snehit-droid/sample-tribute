God Of Hockey

Major Dhyan Chand

The following list is a timeline of Dhyan Chand's Life

  • 1905 - Dhyan Chand was born in Allahabad on 29 August in a Rajput family.
  • 1932 - Chand graduated from Victoria College, Gwalior.
  • 1922 - On 29 August, on his 17th birthday, Chand enlisted in the 1st Brahmans of the British Indian Army as a sepoy (private).
  • 1927 - Two Test matches against the New Zealand squad, the team won the first and narrowly lost the second. Returning to India, Chand was promoted to Lance Naik.
  • 1933 - Chand's home team, the Jhansi Heroes, he participated in and won the Beighton Cup.
  • 1936 - The International Hockey Federation records also attribute only 3 of the 8 goals to Dhyan Chand in the Berlin Olympic final. The final was included in the Leni Riefenstahl film on the 1936 Olympics.
  • 1938 - On 16 March 1938, he was made a Viceroy's Commissioned Officer.
  • 1943 - Chand received an emergency commission as a second lieutenant in the 14th Punjab Regiment, with the war-substantive rank of lieutenant from the same date.
  • 1951 - He was honored at India's National Stadium with the inaugural Dhyan Chand Tournament, which he attended to the admiration of the spectators.
  • 1979 - 3 December 1979 (aged 74), He died at All India Institute of Medical Sciences, New Delhi, India
